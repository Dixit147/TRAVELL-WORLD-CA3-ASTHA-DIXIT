PARIS

Paris is France's capital city and is known for its culture, fashion, food, and history


Location Paris is situated on the Seine River in the north of France, in the center of the Île-de-France region. The city is surrounded by forests of beech and oak, known as the “lungs of Paris”. Size Paris is the fourth largest city in the European Union and the 30th most densely populated city in the world. The city proper is small, with no corner more than about 6 miles (10 km) from the square in front of Notre-Dame Cathedral. History Paris has been a major center of finance, diplomacy, commerce, culture, fashion, and gastronomy since the 17th century. In the 19th century, it became known as the City of Light for its leading role in the arts and sciences, and its early and extensive system of street lighting. Attractions Paris is famous for its many monuments, museums, and the Moulin Rouge. The Seine River is a popular destination for strolling along the wide walkways that line its banks. Some of the city's most famous bridges include the Pont Alexandre III and the Pont Neuf.
